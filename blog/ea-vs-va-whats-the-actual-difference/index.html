EA vs VA, What's the Actual Difference?

October 3, 2024 · 5 min read

This question comes up constantly, and the confusion is understandable. Both titles suggest administrative support. Both can be remote. Both can save you time. But they are fundamentally different roles, and hiring the wrong one for your needs is an expensive mistake.

What a Virtual Assistant does

A Virtual Assistant (VA) typically handles defined, repeatable tasks: data entry, social media scheduling, basic customer service, research, transcription, booking. They execute clearly specified instructions efficiently. The work is largely task-based and doesn't require deep knowledge of your business or judgment-based decision making.

What an Executive Assistant does

An Executive Assistant operates at a higher level of trust, judgment, and strategic awareness. An EA manages your calendar not just by booking meetings, but by protecting your priorities, understanding your goals, and making proactive decisions about your time. An EA manages your inbox not just by responding, but by understanding the nuance of your relationships and communicating in your voice. An EA anticipates needs before they're expressed.

How to know which you need

If you have a list of specific tasks that need executing efficiently, a VA is likely the right fit. If you need someone to think alongside you, manage relationships on your behalf, and take ownership of your operations, you need an Executive Assistant. The investment is higher, but so is the return.
