5 Signs You Need to Hire an Executive Assistant Right Now

March 15, 2025 · 7 min read

If you're reading this article, there's already a good chance you need an Executive Assistant. The fact that you're searching for answers about delegation, time management, or operational support is itself a signal worth paying attention to.

As an Executive Assistant who has worked with founders, creative directors, and business leaders across multiple industries, I've seen the same five patterns appear, always before a client finally makes the decision to get proper support.

1. Your to-do list is longer at the end of the day than it was at the start

This is the most common sign. You're working hard, but the list keeps growing. Tasks that should take minutes are sitting untouched for days because something more urgent always appears. This is what unmanaged operational load looks like, and it's a solvable problem.

2. You're answering emails at midnight

Your inbox has become the default end-of-day activity because there's no space for it during work hours. An Executive Assistant takes full ownership of your inbox, triaging, responding, and escalating only what genuinely needs your attention. Most leaders find this single change saves them 2–3 hours daily.

3. Important things are slipping through the cracks

A missed follow-up. A forgotten deadline. A vendor who needed a response last week. When things start slipping, it's not because you're disorganised, it's because the volume has exceeded what one person can reasonably manage alone.

4. You haven't had a focused, uninterrupted work session in weeks

The kind of work that moves your business forward, strategy, creativity, relationship-building, requires protected time. If your calendar is so full of reactive tasks and unnecessary meetings that focus time has disappeared entirely, it's time for structural support.

5. You're doing work that isn't worth your hourly rate

This is the simplest test. Think about what your time is worth per hour, then look at what you spent the last three hours doing. If you were booking travel, formatting documents, or chasing invoices, that's work an Executive Assistant can handle completely, freeing you to operate at your actual level.

The good news: all five of these problems are entirely solvable. The right Executive Assistant doesn't just take tasks off your plate, they build the systems, rhythms, and structures that prevent the overwhelm from returning.
